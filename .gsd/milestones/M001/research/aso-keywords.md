# ASO Keyword Research: Color Sort Puzzle Games

**Date:** March 22, 2026  
**App:** PourSort  
**Category:** Puzzle Games (App Store)

---

## Summary

The color/water/ball sort puzzle genre is a mature, high-competition segment within mobile puzzle games. Roughly 70% of app installs originate from app store searches, making keyword optimization critical for a new entrant. The genre is dominated by a handful of well-established titles with millions of downloads that aggressively keyword-stuff their metadata. PourSort's naming advantage — the word "pour" — opens a differentiation angle that most competitors don't occupy in their titles.

### Metadata Patterns Observed

1. **Title = Primary Keyword Phrase**: Every top app uses its full 30-character title allowance to pack in the highest-value search terms
2. **Subtitle = Secondary Keywords**: Subtitles like "Color Puzzle Games", "Sort Color", "Color Sorting Game" serve purely as keyword vehicles
3. **Description Keyword Stuffing**: Descriptions repeat core terms (water sort, ball sort, color sort, puzzle, relaxing) dozens of times — Google Play indexes descriptions; App Store does not, but it helps with semantic understanding
4. **"Pour" is underused**: Very few top apps use "pour" in their title/subtitle despite "pour" being the core game action. This is PourSort's biggest keyword gap opportunity.

## 3. Keyword Gap Analysis — Opportunities for PourSort

### Gap 1: "Pour" Keywords (BIGGEST OPPORTUNITY)
- **pour puzzle**, **pour game**, **pour sort**, **water pour**, **color pour**
- Almost ZERO competitors target "pour" in title/subtitle despite it being the literal game mechanic
- "PourSort" naturally captures this entire keyword family
- Users who've played the genre know the action as "pouring" — this is latent search intent waiting to be captured

### Gap 2: Emotional/Benefit Keywords
- **relaxing sort game**, **stress relief puzzle**, **calm puzzle game**, **anxiety relief game**
- Reviews overwhelmingly describe these games in emotional terms: "relaxing", "calming", "like meditation", "helps with anxiety"
- Most competitors waste subtitle space on generic "Color Puzzle Games" instead of benefit-driven keywords
- Opportunity to own the emotional positioning in subtitle/keyword field

### Gap 3: "Offline" + "No Ads" Positioning
- **ad free puzzle**, **no ads sort game**, **offline sort puzzle**
- User reviews are OVERWHELMINGLY negative about ads across ALL top competitors
- If PourSort offers a clean ad experience or premium model, this is a major differentiator
- Users actively search for "ad free" versions of these games

### Gap 4: Accessibility Keywords
- **colorblind puzzle**, **colorblind mode game**
- Multiple reviews across top apps mention inability to play due to color blindness
- Almost no competitor has a colorblind mode or markets one
- Small volume but very low competition and high conversion intent

### Gap 5: Specific Mechanic Variants
- **liquid sort**, **bottle sort**, **tube sort**, **fill the cup**
- Each variant has its own small search audience
- Can be captured in the 100-character keyword field

## 4. iOS App Store Metadata Strategy for PourSort

### Metadata Fields & Character Limits

| Field | Limit | Ranking Weight | Visibility |
|---|---|---|---|
| **App Name (Title)** | 30 characters | Highest | Visible everywhere |
| **Subtitle** | 30 characters | 2nd highest | Visible in search results, below title |
| **Keyword Field** | 100 characters | 3rd highest (hidden) | Not visible to users |
| **Description** | 4,000 characters | Not indexed by Apple (but affects semantics in iOS 26) | Visible on product page |

### Recommended Title (30 chars max)
```
PourSort: Color Sorting Puzzle
```
**Character count:** 30 ✓  
**Keywords captured:** pour, sort, color, sorting, puzzle  
**Rationale:** Brand-first with colon separator (Apple-approved pattern). Captures "color sorting puzzle" and "pour sort" naturally. "PourSort" itself is a compound of "pour" + "sort" — the two most descriptive action words for the genre.

**Alternatives:**
- `PourSort - Water Sort Puzzle` (30 chars) — trades "color sorting" for "water sort" which has higher volume
- `PourSort: Ball & Color Sort` (28 chars) — captures "ball sort" + "color sort"
- `PourSort - Color Sort Game` (27 chars) — simpler, captures "color sort game"

### Recommended Subtitle (30 chars max)
```
Water Sort & Relaxing Tube Fun
```
**Character count:** 30 ✓  
**Keywords captured:** water, sort, relaxing, tube, fun  
**Rationale:** Captures "water sort" (highest-volume keyword), "tube" (genre descriptor), and "relaxing" (emotional benefit keyword that competitors ignore in subtitles). "Fun" is a natural engagement word.

**Alternatives:**
- `Water Sort & Tube Puzzle Game` (30 chars) — more keyword-dense but less emotionally engaging
- `Relaxing Ball & Liquid Puzzle` (30 chars) — captures "ball", "liquid", "relaxing"
- `Ball Sort & Water Puzzle Game` (30 chars) — hits both "ball sort" and "water puzzle"

### Recommended Keyword Field (100 chars, comma-separated, no spaces after commas)
```
ball sort,pour game,liquid sort,bottle puzzle,brain,offline,color match,calm,sorting,ad free,no wifi
```
**Character count:** ~99 ✓  
**Rules applied:**
- No duplicates with title/subtitle (Apple de-dupes automatically but wastes chars)
- No plurals (Apple auto-matches singular/plural)
- No "game" or "puzzle" (already in title/subtitle)
- Comma-separated, no spaces after commas (maximizes character usage)
- Single words where possible (Apple combines across fields)

## 5. Description Strategy

Apple doesn't index the description for keyword ranking, but iOS 26 introduces AI-powered tags that analyze descriptions and screenshots for semantic categorization. Write the description to:

1. **First 3 lines are critical** — only these are visible before "Read More" tap
2. **Lead with benefit, not features**: "Sort colorful liquids into matching tubes in this relaxing, ad-light puzzle game"
3. **Use natural keyword language** throughout (not keyword-stuffed)
4. **Emphasize differentiators**: ad-light/premium experience, colorblind mode (if built), offline play, no timers
5. **Include emotional language from user reviews**: relaxing, calming, satisfying, stress relief, brain training

### First 3 Lines (Most Critical)
> PourSort is a beautifully simple color sorting puzzle. Pour colored liquids between tubes until each tube holds a single color. Relaxing, satisfying, and designed for your peace of mind — no aggressive ads, no pressure.

## 6. Visual ASO Notes

- **App Icon**: Use bold, recognizable tube/bottle imagery with 2-3 vibrant colors. Avoid cluttered designs. The icon is the single biggest conversion factor after the title.
- **Screenshots**: First 3 screenshots are most important. Show gameplay (not menus), include short captions with benefit keywords ("Relaxing Color Sort", "1000+ Puzzles", "No Timer, No Pressure").
- **Color Palette**: Stand out from competitors who mostly use blue/green gradients. Consider warm tones or a distinctive style.

## 7. Recommended ASO Launch Strategy

### Phase 1: Launch (Week 1-4)
- **Target low-competition long-tails** to build initial ranking signal
- Focus on: pour puzzle, pour game, liquid sort, bottle puzzle, tube sort
- Goal: Rank top 10 for 5+ long-tail keywords

### Phase 2: Growth (Month 2-3)
- **Shift subtitle toward higher-volume terms** once you have download velocity
- A/B test subtitle: "Water Sort & Relaxing Tube Fun" vs "Water & Ball Sort Puzzle"
- Add seasonal keywords to keyword field (holiday, christmas during Q4)
- Goal: Rank top 20 for "color sort" and "sorting game"

### Phase 3: Scale (Month 4+)
- **Target head terms** with established ranking authority
- Rotate keyword field monthly based on ranking performance
- Consider localization (translate keywords for top 5 non-English markets)
- Goal: Rank top 50 for "water sort" and "ball sort"

### Ongoing
- Monitor keyword rankings weekly using App Store Connect analytics (Impressions, Product Page Views)
- Track conversion rate by keyword — high impressions but low conversions means the keyword attracts wrong audience
- Update keywords with every app update (typically bi-weekly)
- Respond to ALL reviews (improves engagement signal)

## 8. Key Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Dominated by established players with millions of reviews | Hard to rank for head terms initially | Focus on long-tail "pour" keywords; build review count aggressively |
| Apple rejects keyword-heavy title | Delayed launch | Title reads naturally ("PourSort: Color Sorting Puzzle") — not stuffed |
| iOS 26 AI tags change ranking factors | Unknown impact on strategy | Write description for semantic quality, not just keywords |
| Genre saturation reduces organic discovery | Lower ceiling for downloads | Differentiate on quality (no ads, colorblind, haptics) not just keywords |
| Ad-heavy competitors have higher ARPU | Less budget for UA | Premium/ad-light model attracts higher-quality users who rate higher |
