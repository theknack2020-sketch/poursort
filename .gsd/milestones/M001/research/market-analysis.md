# Color Sort / Ball Sort Puzzle — iOS Market Analysis

**Date:** March 2026  
**Sources:** AppMagic, Sensor Tower, GameAnalytics, Business of Apps, Statista, Udonis, MAF, Verified Market Reports, 360 Research Reports

---

## Executive Summary

The mobile puzzle market is a $8–12B+ annual revenue category and one of the fastest-growing casual game segments. Within it, the **Sort Puzzle sub-genre** (ball sort, color sort, screw puzzles) is small but exploding — doubling IAP revenue since 2024 to ~$279M in 2025. Sort Puzzle is also a world leader in download growth. The broader casual puzzle market is mature and dominated by Match-3 giants, but Sort/Fill/Block puzzle niches are where new entrants are finding traction. The genre skews female (≈75% for puzzle broadly), ages 25–44, with short frequent sessions. Retention is strong relative to other mobile genres.

## 4. User Demographics

### Gender
- Mobile gaming overall: ~53.6% male, 46.4% female
- **Puzzle games specifically: ~75% female** — one of the most gender-skewed categories
- Women are more likely to be mobile-only gamers (44% of women vs 27% of men)
- Women tend to prefer word, puzzle, board, and trivia games

### Age
- **Core puzzle demographic: ages 25–44** (over half the audience)
- Average mobile gamer age: 36 (overall), 41 (Global Video Games Report)
- Gen X (1965–1980) and Baby Boomers: **prefer puzzle and arcade** over all other genres
- Millennials: enjoy puzzle alongside adventure/shooter
- Players 55+: clear preference for single-player puzzle games
- Younger audiences (Gen Alpha, Gen Z) prefer adventure/shooter/fighting

### Income & Motivation
- The 25–44 age bracket has high disposable income, making them attractive for IAP
- Primary motivations: boredom relief, stress reduction, cognitive stimulation
- 63% of mobile puzzle gamers report playing at least once per week
- 74% of US mobile gamers will watch a video ad for in-app content

- Only 4 out of 367 newly launched Match-3 titles surpassed $100K monthly revenue in 2025

## 7. Monetization Patterns

### Revenue Models
- **77% of mobile game revenue comes from IAP** (casual titles driving majority)
- Hybrid model (IAP + rewarded ads) is standard for casual puzzle
- 82% of players prefer free games with ads over paid games without
- Rewarded video ads: +27% higher conversions than static banners in puzzle

### User Acquisition
- Average CPI for casual games: ~$1 (2023 baseline)
- iOS CPI: $2.23 (significantly higher than Android)
- Android CPI: $0.63
- UA costs for puzzle dropped 12% YoY in 2024 due to stronger organic discovery
- D7 ROAS for puzzle: ~6.9% (slightly behind simulation at 8.5% and lifestyle at 8.3%)

### Revenue Per User
- Casual games generally have lower ARPDAU than RPGs
- US ARPU (mobile gaming overall): ~$60.58 in 2025
- Sort puzzle games monetize through: ads (interstitial + rewarded), IAP for hints/undo/extra tubes, remove-ads purchase, cosmetics

## 8. Sort Puzzle Market Position

### Size Relative to Puzzle Market

| Level | Revenue | Sort Puzzle Share |
|-------|---------|------------------|
| Total mobile games | $57.1B | 0.49% |
| Total puzzle genre | $8.2–12.2B | 2.3–3.4% |
| Hybridcasual puzzle niche | ~$1B (Sort + Screw + Block + Fill) | ~28% of niche |

### Growth vs. Established Segments

Sort Puzzle is in a fundamentally different competitive position than Match-3:
- **Match-3**: $4.8B market, flat growth, 4/367 new titles crossed $100K/month — extremely hard to break in
- **Sort Puzzle**: $279M market, 2× YoY growth, still early — much easier to gain traction as a new entrant
- **Block Puzzle**: $213M, 10× YoY growth — also explosive but getting crowded (Block Blast dominance)

### Competitive Advantages for New Entrants in Sort Puzzle
1. **Lower barrier to entry** than Match-3 (no need to compete with $1.3B Royal Match)
2. **Genre still fragmenting** — no single dominant title like Royal Match equivalent
3. **Download growth leader** — organic discovery is strong
4. **Simple core mechanic** — low development cost, fast iteration
5. **Strong retention characteristics** — puzzle genre inherently retains well at D7+

### Risks & Challenges
1. **Clone saturation** — hundreds of near-identical ball sort apps already exist
2. **Low ARPDAU** — sort puzzle games monetize less aggressively than Match-3 or casino
3. **Revenue ceiling** — even top sort games reach ~$1M/week, not $1M/day like Royal Match
4. **User review fatigue** — multiple reviews cite repetitive gameplay and ad overload as top complaints
5. **Mature game dominance** — 90%+ of puzzle revenue goes to games >2 years old

## 9. Strategic Implications for PourSort

### Market Opportunity
The sort puzzle sub-genre is at an inflection point — significant download growth, doubling revenue, but not yet consolidated. The window for differentiation is now, before a clear category winner emerges.

### Differentiation Vectors (based on user complaints and market gaps)
1. **Variety** — Users complain about repetitive 4-ball puzzles; variable ball counts, tube sizes, and mechanics can address this
2. **Ad experience** — Heavy ad load is the #1 negative review topic; premium/subscription model or tasteful ad integration could differentiate
3. **Progression depth** — Layer meta-game, narrative, or collectible elements on top of sort mechanics (hybridcasual trend)
4. **Visual polish** — Most sort games have generic aesthetics; distinctive art direction stands out
5. **Accessibility** — Target the 55+ demographic specifically underserved in sort puzzle

### Monetization Strategy Implications
- Hybrid IAP + rewarded ads is the proven model
- Remove-ads IAP ($3–5 one-time) is common
- Hint/undo/extra-tube IAPs drive recurring spend
- Weekly/seasonal events boost D28+ retention significantly
- Daily challenges + login rewards are table stakes
